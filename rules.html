Welcome to the rules of blackjack!


In blackjack, the objective is to beat the dealer's hand without going over 21.


Each card has a value:

  • Cards 2 through 10 are worth their face value.
  • Face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are each worth 10 points.
  • Aces are worth either 1 or 11 points, depending on what's more advantageous for the player's hand.

The game begins with each player, being dealt two cards. The dealer has one card face up and one card face down.


Players then have the option to "hit" (receive another card from the dealer) or "stand" (keep their current hand). If a player's hand value exceeds 21, they bust and lose the game.


Once all players have chosen to stand, the dealer reveals their face-down card and must hit until their hand reaches a value of 17 or higher.


If the dealer busts, remaining players win. If neither busts, the one with the highest hand value wins. In a tie, it's a push, and the player's bet is returned.


Special plays include:

  • Split: If a player's first two cards are of the same value, they can split them into two separate hands.
  • Double Down: Players may double their original bet and receive one more card.
  • Insurance: Available when the dealer's face-up card is an Ace, it's a side bet that pays 2:1 if the dealer has a blackjack.

A player gets a blackjack if their first two cards are an Ace and a 10-value card. They typically win 1.5 times their bet unless the dealer also has a blackjack, resulting in a push.


To win:

  • Have a hand closer to 21 than the dealer's without exceeding it.
  • If the dealer's hand is closer to 21, the player loses their bet.
  • In case of a tie, the player's bet is returned.
